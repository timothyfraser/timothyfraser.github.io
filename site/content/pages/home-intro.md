I'm a **computational social scientist** and political methodologist applying data science and mixed methods to help communities **combat and adapt to climate change**.

My research asks why some cities produce better environmental, transportation, health, and resilience outcomes than others. I focus on local-level policy and community resources — social ties, social infrastructure, and the networks that let residents mobilize collective action.

At Cornell, I lead software development for the **Climate Action in Transportation (CAT)** team with Dr. Oliver Gao, building dashboards, databases, APIs, and cloud jobs so local decision-makers can estimate emissions online — without an EPA-MOVES install. I also teach **System Reliability & Six Sigma** and run a growing roster of MEng research projects on congestion pricing, ML emissions surrogates, and global bikeshare networks.

Since 2017 I've published **44+ peer-reviewed studies** on US and Japanese cities, collaborated with **18+ scholars** and **34+ students**, and consulted for the UN Development Programme's Accelerator Labs in Mexico and Paraguay. Past work has been funded by a 2016 Fulbright fellowship and a US-DOT research center grant.
