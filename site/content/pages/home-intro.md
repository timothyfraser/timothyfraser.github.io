Dr. Timothy (Tim) Fraser is an Assistant Teaching Professor in the Systems Engineering Program at Cornell University, and Coordinator for the Center for Transportation, Environment, and Community Health (CTECH). He is a computational social scientist, developing methods, systems, and software to help communities combat environmental crises.

He has published 50+ peer-reviewed studies, plus dashboards and software, on urban resilience and environmental systems in the US and Japan. His research has been featured in major media outlets, including the *New York Times*, *Vox*, *Bloomberg News*, and more, and has been funded by multiple national grants, including Fulbright.

He has advised policy on social infrastructure at the OECD and the UN Development Programme's Accelerator Labs, and earned his PhD from Northeastern University in 2022. His current research focuses on building AI and cloud computing tools to quantify, visualize, and communicate environmental policy data to the public, to support community action and resilience in socio-technical systems.
